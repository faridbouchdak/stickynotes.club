# Create and manage a private board

Use a private board when an idea needs more space. Keep it as your own workspace, or invite people when you are ready to develop the idea together.

## What you'll learn

- How to create a board and choose a template.
- Who can open and contribute to a board.
- How to add and arrange sticky notes.
- What happens when you delete a board.

## Create a board

Every board is private from the start, so you do not need to choose a visibility setting.

1. Select **New board**.
2. Enter a **Name**.
3. Add a **Description** if it will help you or your Participants.
4. Choose a template. **Blank** is selected by default.
5. Create the board and add your first sticky note.

## Choose a useful starting point

Templates give your board an initial structure. You can keep changing the board as your ideas develop.

| Template | Starting structure |
| --- | --- |
| **Blank** | An empty grid that can later change to columns |
| **Retro** | Columns for Went well, To improve and Actions |
| **Kanban** | Columns for To do, Doing and Done |
| **Week planner** | A column for every weekday |
| **Brainstorm** | An open grid with a different default background; it can later change to columns |

You can choose from nine backgrounds. Boards grow as you add material, but you cannot duplicate a board.

## Decide who can take part

Every board has one **Owner**. Ownership cannot be transferred or reassigned.

A person you invite becomes a **Participant** after accepting. Participants can see the complete board, create sticky notes, edit and delete their own sticky notes, add and delete their own comments, use hearts and take part in dot voting.

Access always starts with an email invitation. The recipient signs in or creates an account before joining. There are no Viewer, Editor or Commenter roles, anonymous visitors, guest access or private-board share links.

See [Work together on a board](/collaboration/) for invitations and access.

## Add and arrange sticky notes

Each sticky note belongs to this board only. It can contain one optional image of up to **5 MB**. The image has the same audience as the board and is deleted with its sticky note.

Reorder sticky notes in a grid, or move them within and between columns. Use tags or visible labels when a shared meaning matters. Colour can add expression, but it has no built-in meaning for status or priority.

## Delete a board

Delete a board only when nobody needs its contents anymore. After confirmation, deletion is immediate and permanent. It deletes every sticky note, comment, heart and dot vote, removes Participant access and cancels pending invitations. Participant accounts are not deleted.

There is no Undo, trash or recovery period. You also cannot archive a whole board as an ordinary board action.

Deleting the Owner's account permanently deletes every board that account owns. Because ownership cannot be transferred, there is no successor Owner.

## Good to know

- Private means access-controlled. A Participant can still copy information they are allowed to see.
- Authorised platform moderators and administrators may access private content only when moderation, support, security or legal work genuinely requires it.
- You cannot move or copy a Public sticky note directly into a private board.
- Opening or viewing a board does not change its content.
